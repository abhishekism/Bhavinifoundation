Legal Empowerment

Know Your Rights

Laws that protect you — explained simply, in plain language.

POCSO Act, 2012 — Protection of Children from Sexual Offences

POCSO protects children under 18 from sexual abuse, harassment, and pornography. It mandates child-friendly procedures and strict punishment for offenders.

  • Any sexual act with a child is a criminal offence regardless of consent
  • Police must record statements in a child-friendly environment
  • Child's identity must be kept confidential throughout the process
  • Failure to report known abuse is also a punishable offence

If you suspect abuse:

  1. 1Call 1098 (Childline) or 112 immediately
  2. 2Contact Bhavini Foundation for guidance
  3. 3File FIR at your local police station
  4. 4Request a Special Juvenile Police Unit officer

Domestic Violence Act, 2005 — Protection of Women

This Act protects women from physical, emotional, sexual, verbal, and economic abuse by any family member or partner. It gives women the right to stay in their home.

  • Right to reside in the shared household, even without ownership
  • Right to protection orders, residence orders, and monetary relief
  • Covers physical, verbal, emotional, and economic abuse
  • Court can issue emergency protection orders within 3 days

How to file a complaint:

  1. 1Contact a Protection Officer (available at District Court)
  2. 2File a Domestic Incident Report (DIR)
  3. 3Approach Magistrate for Protection Order
  4. 4Call Bhavini — we'll accompany you through the process

Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961 & IPC Section 498A

Giving or taking dowry is a criminal offence. IPC 498A protects married women from cruelty, harassment for dowry, and abetment of suicide by in-laws.

Dowry Prohibition Act

Punishment: up to 5 years imprisonment and fine of ₹15,000 or the value of dowry, whichever is higher.

IPC Section 498A

Cruelty by husband or relatives — imprisonment up to 3 years + fine. Cognisable and non-bailable.

IPC Section 304B

Dowry death — if a woman dies within 7 years of marriage under suspicious circumstances, minimum 7 years imprisonment.

ITPA & IPC Section 370 — Anti-Human Trafficking Laws

IPC Section 370 criminalises all forms of human trafficking — for forced labour, sexual exploitation, or organ harvesting. Trafficking of children carries stricter punishment.

  • Trafficker: minimum 7 years, up to life imprisonment
  • Trafficking of children: minimum 10 years imprisonment
  • Victims have right to free legal aid and rehabilitation
  • Victim's statement recorded in private with female officer

Report Trafficking

Call 1800-111-565 (anti-trafficking helpline) or contact Bhavini Foundation immediately. We coordinate with police, NGOs, and shelter homes for rescue and rehabilitation.

Everything you need to know before you start — from training to code of conduct.

Your Journey as a Volunteer

1

Registration & Screening

Submit your registration form. Our team will review your skills and availability and get back within 3–5 working days.

2

Orientation Session

Attend a 2-hour online orientation covering our mission, the communities we serve, code of conduct, and confidentiality guidelines.

3

Role-Specific Training

Depending on your role — legal aid, counselling support, awareness campaigns, or social media — you'll receive focused training from our team.

4

Field Assignment

Get assigned to a project team. Work alongside experienced staff on real programs in communities, schools, or legal support contexts.

5

Ongoing Support & Recognition

Monthly volunteer meetups, impact updates, certificates of appreciation, and opportunities to grow into leadership roles.

Code of Conduct

  • Maintain strict confidentiality of all survivor information

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get help from Bhavini Foundation?

Call us on +91 8448928049 or +91 8448928452, or email info@bhavinifoundation.com. Our team will assess your situation and connect you with the right support — counselling, legal aid, or shelter — without any fees or judgement.

Is my information kept confidential?

Absolutely. All personal information shared with Bhavini Foundation is strictly confidential. We never share your details without your explicit consent, except in cases of immediate danger to life where legal obligations apply.

How can I refer someone in need?

Call our helpline directly. You can refer a friend, neighbour, or family member by sharing their situation with our team. We'll reach out sensitively and offer support. You don't need the person's permission to make a referral — our team will handle it carefully.

How are donations used?

68% goes directly to program costs (counselling, legal aid, workshops), 18% to community outreach, 9% to administration, and 5% to our future shelter home fund. We publish annual impact reports. All donations are eligible for 50% tax deduction under Section 80G.

Do you offer services in Hindi?

Yes. All our services, workshops, and helpline support are available in Hindi. We work primarily in Uttar Pradesh and understand the linguistic and cultural needs of the communities we serve.

Can organisations partner with Bhavini Foundation?

Yes. We actively seek partnerships with schools, hospitals, corporations (for CSR), and NGOs working in aligned areas. Email us at info@bhavinifoundation.com with "Partnership Enquiry" in the subject line and we'll get back within 2 working days.

How do I get a tax exemption certificate for my donation?

After making a donation, email your transaction details and PAN number to info@bhavinifoundation.com. We'll issue an 80G receipt within 7 working days, which you can use to claim 50% tax deduction on your donation amount.

Still have questions? We're here.

Call us, email us, or WhatsApp — our team responds within 24 hours.
