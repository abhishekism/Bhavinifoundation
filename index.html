Who We Are

We Don't Just Help.
We Transform Lives.

Founded in 2024 in Faizabad, Uttar Pradesh, Bhavini Foundation was born from an urgent reality — that thousands of women and children in our communities live in fear of domestic violence, dowry harassment, sexual exploitation, and trafficking, with nowhere to turn.

We bridge that gap. Through professional counselling, legal advocacy, POCSO awareness in schools, menstrual health education, and direct intervention — we give survivors not just support, but power.

"We treat the person, not just the problem — addressing the biological, psychological, and social factors that keep people trapped."

Our Programs Support Us

Government Advocacy

Partnering with the Ministry of Women & Child Development, local police, and legal bodies to strengthen protection systems for women and children.

Rural Accessibility

Conducting workshops in schools, health clinics, and community centres — reaching the most underserved corners of UP through local partnerships.

Policy Change

Lobbying for stronger laws on domestic violence, child abuse, and trafficking — building coalitions that create lasting systemic change beyond individual cases.

What We Do

Programs That Actually Work

Each program is built around a survivor's full journey — from crisis to confidence.

Mental Health Care

Professional counselling, trauma therapy, and mental health first aid for survivors of domestic violence, sexual abuse, and trafficking — delivered by trained clinical staff.

  • Individual & group counselling
  • Mental health first aid training
  • Rural community outreach

Legal Aid & Justice

Free legal support, POCSO Act workshops in schools, domestic violence awareness sessions, and direct advocacy for survivors navigating a complex legal system.

  • Free legal consultation
  • POCSO awareness in schools
  • Good Touch / Bad Touch workshops

Rescue & Rehabilitation

Active intervention for trafficking and exploitation survivors, paired with long-term rehabilitation, vocational skill-building, and community reintegration support.

  • Crisis intervention & safe shelter
  • Vocational skill-building
  • Social reintegration support

School Workshops

Age-appropriate, interactive workshops delivered directly in schools across UP — equipping children and teachers with the knowledge to recognise, report, and respond to abuse.

  • POCSO Act awareness sessions
  • Good Touch / Bad Touch for children
  • Menstrual hygiene education for girls
  • Teacher sensitisation training

Community Sessions

Grassroots awareness drives in villages, urban slums, and community centres — bringing critical information on domestic violence, dowry rights, and mental health to those who need it most.

  • Domestic violence awareness drives
  • Women's legal rights workshops
  • Mental health destigmatisation talks
  • Rural health & hygiene camps

Collaborations

We don't work in silos. We actively partner with organisations working in parallel spaces — amplifying collective impact through shared expertise, resources, and reach.

  • Hospitals & women's health NGOs
  • Ministry of Women & Child Dev.
  • Corporate CSR partnerships
  • UNICEF & UN Women (planned)

Our Roadmap

Where We're Headed

1–2 Years

Short-Term Goals

  • POCSO & Safety Workshops — Expand to more schools and community centres across UP.

  • Strategic Partnerships — Formalise collaborations with hospitals, schools, and government bodies.

  • Fundraising Campaigns — Crowdfunding, charity events, and corporate CSR partnerships.

  • Volunteer Corps — Train 100+ volunteers in mental health first aid and legal support.

3–5 Years

Long-Term Vision

  • Shelter Homes — Build safe, legally supported shelters for women escaping violence and trafficking.

  • National Expansion — Extend reach through state-level campaigns, online webinars, and national NGO partnerships.

  • Policy Influence — Drive legislative change on domestic violence, trafficking, and child protection laws.

  • Global Partnerships — Collaborate with UNICEF, UN Women, and international human rights bodies.
